The Carbon Ledger

A public record of every European emission allowance, since 2021
Cover · Issue 14
Europe’s carbon market, written down in full view.
The European Emissions Trading System has been the continent’s primary climate instrument for two decades. From this week, every allowance, every trade, and every retirement is published the moment it is recorded — readable by anyone, without registration, in 24 official languages. Since the first allowance was issued in January 2024 under Phase IV, 4,503 EUA have entered circulation across twelve verified emitters and buyers. Forty-seven trades have settled to date. No allowance has yet been retired. This issue takes you through the data in plain English, and explains how to read the on-chain ledger that sits beneath it.
